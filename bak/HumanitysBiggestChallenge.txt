There's an article on vice.com that makes a great example of what I believe to be the greatest, most difficult, and potentially most rewarding challenge we as human beings can face. The article is about smartphones, but that's not the challenge.

The challenge is to consider that you might be wrong. About everything. 

Around 2017, people started mentioning that they suspected Facebook's smartphone app of listening to their conversations to serve targeted ads. If you're reading this online, I'm sure you've heard it by now - maybe even experienced the phenomenon yourself. Specifics vary, but they all go something like this: two people are talking out loud, and then later one of them sees an ad related to the same subject.
